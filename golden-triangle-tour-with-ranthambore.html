Tour Plan

Overview

With excursions to Ranthambore and the Golden Triangle offered by White Umbrella, explore India's rich cultural heritage and vast array of animals. This eight-day itinerary completes the round of the Golden Triangle by visiting the well-known towns of Delhi, Agra, and Jaipur. You will be able to see the Taj Mahal in all of its magnificent splendor, one of the world's seven wonders. Enjoy discovering Jaipur's attractions. See the infamous Ranthambore National Park after that. If you happen to spot a Royal Bengal Tiger there, you'll feel a rush of thrill.

There will be stops at gardens, museums, planetariums, theme parks, and landmarks along the route. This meticulously designed Golden Triangle package with a Ranthambore itinerary includes the greatest places for sightseeing, food, hotel, and transportation.

Tour Itinerary

When you arrive in Delhi, our tour coordinator will be happy to welcome you and make arrangements for a relaxing transfer to your lodging. Once you have completed the check-in process upon arrival, spend the rest of the day at leisure. Benefit from a complimentary one-night stay at the hotel.

Proceed for the sightseeing tour of Delhi after breakfast. View Qutub Minar, the tallest brick minaret in the world. See the tomb of Humayun, the Mughal emperor who provided the architectural model for the Taj Mahal.

Proceed on to the Birla Mandir, which is also referred to as the Lakshmi Narayan Temple. Spend some time here before continuing to one of Delhi's most well-known sites, the India Gate. Spend the afternoon strolling around Old Delhi and shopping at Chandni Chowk, Delhi's oldest shopping area. Rajghat and Jama Masjid are also worthwhile visits. Return to the hotel by dusk so you can spend the night there.

Start the third day of your Ranthambore Golden Triangle Tour with a hearty breakfast. Take a 4-hour road transfer from Delhi to Agra after checking out. When you get there, you'll see the Taj Mahal, a white marble mausoleum representing love. Particularly on nights with a full moon, the edifice has a stunning aspect at dawn and twilight. Once the visit is over, head to the hotel, settle in, and make yourself at home in bed.

Awaken for a wonderful breakfast. After checking out, take a 6-hour flight from Agra to Ranthambore. Savor a pit stop at Fatehpur Sikri en route. Buland Darwaza, Jama Masjid, and the Tomb of Salim Chishti are important locations nearby. Other places worth seeing are Panch Mahal, Birbal's Palace, and Mariam-uz-Zamani's Palace.

Proceed with your drive toward Ranthambore. The Jaipur royal family used Ranthambore National Park, one of the most well-known national parks in India, as a hunting reserve. Once you're there, check into your hotel and enjoy a peaceful evening.

Take a wildlife safari at Ranthambore in the morning. You can see a range of animal and plant species coexisting peacefully in the park's natural environment. Feel the rush of excitement when you spot a tiger sniffing around. After the exhilarating encounter, return to the hotel in time for breakfast. After some rest, get ready for the park's afternoon safari.

After admiring the national park's splendor, return to the hotel to spend the night.

On the sixth day of your Golden Triangle holiday with Ranthambore, after a hearty breakfast, proceed to Jaipur. Once you've reached the hotel in Jaipur, make yourself at home for the rest of the day. Jaipur, sometimes known as the "Pink City," is well known for its magnificent architecture, friendly residents, deep cultural traditions, and stunning vistas that make for an amazing trip. Go to bed early in the hotel later.

After breakfast, get ready for the sightseeing tour of Jaipur. Begin your adventure by visiting Amber Fort. Here, you can see Sheesh Mahal's magnificent mirror work and carvings. Head over to Hawa Mahal, the "Palace of Wind," which is a five-story building with 953 windows.

Proceed towards City Palace, which is largely responsible for Jaipur's popularity, and Jantar Mantar, also known as the Royal Observatory, a UNESCO World Heritage Site that holds a range of astronomical instruments for tracking space occurrences. After that, return to the hotel and get a full night's sleep to help you forget how tired you are.

Today is the last day of the Golden Triangle tour with Ranthambore. After a leisurely breakfast, check out and drive to Delhi, which is 5.5 hours away. From there, you can take a train or fly home, filled with fond memories.
